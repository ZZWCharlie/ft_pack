Overall Water Splitting (OWS) — Experimental Extraction Hierarchy

0. Paper metadata
Title; authors; journal; year; DOI; figure/table IDs referenced in extraction.

1. Photocatalyst system & architecture
1.1 OWS mode: one-step vs two-step (Z-scheme).
1.2 Components:
•	(One-step) Photocatalyst identity.
•	(Z-scheme) H2-evolution photocatalyst (HEP), O2-evolution photocatalyst (OEP), mediator type (redox pair & conc.; or solid mediator—material, loading, placement).
1.3 Intended light range (UV/vis/NIR; λ cutoff).
1.4 Safety/separation approach for H2/O2 (compartments, membranes, headspace purge).

2. Composition & crystal chemistry (emphasis on HEO where relevant)
2.1 Nominal composition/formula; cations with site assignment and molar fractions (equimolar vs non-equimolar).
2.2 Target crystal structure: rock-salt (B1), spinel (AB₂O₄), perovskite (ABO₃), fluorite (AO₂), other; space group.
2.3 Element-selection rationale: ionic radii spread, oxidation states (d⁰–d¹⁰), electronegativity spread, tolerance/Goldschmidt metrics (if given).
2.4 Dopants/solid solutions: element, site, amount (at.% or wt.%), purpose (band tuning/defects).
2.5 Defects (as-claimed and as-measured): oxygen vacancies, metal vacancies, antisites; quant/qual metrics (EPR signal, XPS O 1s deconvolution, positron, EXAFS).
2.6 Cation disorder/entropy indicators (mixing entropy, configurational parameter) if reported.

3. Band structure & energetics (capture values + measurement method)
3.1 Band gap Eg (eV) and method (Tauc/DRS, PL).
3.2 CBM/VBM (vs RHE, NHE, Ag/AgCl, or vacuum); work function/UPS; band alignment across junctions.
3.3 Built-in field/ferroelectric/p–n/Schottky/phase-junction details; evidence (KPFM/SPV/Mott-Schottky/TA/PL quench).
3.4 Fermi level, carrier type/density (Mott-Schottky slope), mobility (if any).

4. Morphology, facets & heterostructures
4.1 Particle size/shape/thickness; facet indices ({hkl}); porosity (BET SA, PSD).
4.2 Crystal phase control (polymorphs, % phase fractions).
4.3 Heterostructure type: core–shell, phase junction, p–n, metal–semiconductor (Schottky), graded composition; interface thickness (nm), coverage (%).
4.4 Substrate/sheet construction (if used): support, binder, thickness, geometric area and roughness factor.

5. Co-catalysts & surface over-layers
5.1 HER co-catalyst: identity, loading (wt.% or nm), dispersion (size, #/µm²), deposition method (photo-dep, impregnation, ALD, etc.), thermal treatment.
5.2 OER co-catalyst: same fields.
5.3 Protective/selectivity layers (e.g., Cr₂O₃, TiO₂): material, thickness, coverage, purpose (suppress HOR/ORR/back reaction).
5.4 Anchoring/interface modifiers (e.g., MgO, CoOx): identity, location, rationale.

6. Synthesis & post-treatments
6.1 Route (solid-state, sol–gel, hydro/solvothermal, combustion, ball-milling, nitridation, annealing, flux).
6.2 Precursors/reagents (chemical names, hydrate state, purity, supplier).
6.3 Stoichiometry & concentrations; solvent(s); pH or mineralizer.
6.4 Temperature profile(s): ramp, peak T, dwell, cooling; atmosphere (air, O₂, N₂, Ar, H₂/NH₃), pressure.
6.5 Post-treatments: reduction/oxidation, defect engineering (e.g., H₂/Ar to make V_O), proton exchange, acid/base wash.
6.6 Co-catalyst deposition details (solution, light intensity/time for photodep., calcination).

7. Characterization (report instrument + key settings)
7.1 Structure: XRD/Rietveld (λ, step, 2θ range), lattice params; Raman.
7.2 Composition/valence: XPS/UPS (source, pass energy), ICP-OES/MS (digest recipe), XAS/EXAFS (beamline, k-range).
7.3 Morphology: SEM/TEM/HRTEM/SAED (kV, magnification), STEM-EDS maps.
7.4 Surface/porosity: N₂ sorption (degas T/time, BET range), Hg porosimetry.
7.5 Photophysics/charge: PL/TRPL (excitation λ, lifetimes), transient absorption (probe window), SPV/KPFM, photocurrent, EIS (freq range), Mott-Schottky (freq, slope).
7.6 Band positions: Tauc analysis settings; flat-band → CBM mapping assumptions.
7.7 Defect probes: EPR (g-values), positron, thermogravimetry for oxygen non-stoichiometry.

8. Reactor & testing configuration
8.1 Reactor type (suspension, fixed-bed, sheet reactor), volume, material, headspace volume; stirring/flow.
8.2 Gas handling: purge gas and rate; H₂/O₂ separation/collection; leak test; sampling protocol; GC/GC-MS settings (column, carrier, calibration).
8.3 Temperature control (bath/setpoint), pO₂/pH₂ (if controlled).
8.4 For Z-scheme: mediator species & concentration; solid mediator placement; compartmentalization.

9. Illumination & optical metrics
9.1 Light source (type/model), spectrum/filters (e.g., AM1.5G, band-pass), irradiance or photon flux (with actinometry method), illuminated area, distance, incidence angle.
9.2 For AQY/IPCE: wavelength(s), bandwidth, chopping, formula used.

10. Reaction medium
10.1 Water source (resistivity/TOC); electrolyte/buffer (identity, concentration); pH (method).
10.2 Atmosphere during testing (air, O₂, N₂, Ar); note ORR risk in air.
10.3 No sacrificial agents (confirm). Additives (e.g., anti-foam) if any.

11. Performance metrics (report units and normalization basis)
11.1 Gas evolution rates: H₂ and O₂ (µmol h⁻¹ g⁻¹ or per cm²); time traces; H₂:O₂ ratio (target 2:1).
11.2 AQY/IPCE: value(s) with wavelength window and calculation; internal/external quantum efficiencies if given.
11.3 STH: value, calculation basis (HHV/LHV), spectral/sunlight conditions.
11.4 Onset/induction time; transient vs steady state; turnover numbers/frequencies (if active-site data exist).
11.5 Areal vs gravimetric normalization for sheets.

12. Selectivity, back-reactions & side pathways
12.1 Measures against HOR and ORR (e.g., Cr₂O₃ overlayers, N₂ purge, membrane).
12.2 Verification that O₂ derives from water (e.g., isotope H₂¹⁸O tests).
12.3 Evidence of reverse reactions/recombination (e.g., O₂ consumption in dark, H₂ decay).
12.4 If DFT/descriptor data are provided: ΔG_H* (HER), ΔG_OH*, ΔG_OOH*, ΔG_O* (OER); identify facet/site modeled.

13. Stability & durability
13.1 Continuous operation time, cycling protocol, average/decayed rates.
13.2 Post-reaction characterization (phase, valence, morphology, vacancy content).
13.3 Leaching/corrosion (ICP of liquor); reusability/regeneration steps.

14. Controls & validation
14.1 Dark/light-off controls; blank reactor; sacrificial agent controls (if any); separate HER/OER tests.
14.2 Gas-tightness and mass-balance checks; calibration curves; duplicated runs and error bars.

15. Data, software & reproducibility
15.1 Number of repeats; statistics; error reporting.
15.2 Data-processing software (versions) for XRD/Rietveld, spectroscopy, AQY/STH.
15.3 Raw data/CIFs/SI/DOIs.
15.4 Safety notes (H₂/O₂ mixtures; venting; PPE; interlocks).
