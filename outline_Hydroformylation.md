Hydroformylation — Experimental Extraction Hierarchy

1. Substrate(s)
   1.1 Identity & class: common name; class (α-olefin, internal olefin, diene, vinyl arene, allyl derivative, vinyl ether/ester/amide, fatty derivative, mixture); SMILES/InChI.
   1.2 Source & composition: commercial, synthesized, refinery cut/FT/SHOP/bio; mixture breakdown (% isomers).
   1.3 Impurities & pre-treatment: butadiene/alkynes/S/Cl/peroxides/water; drying/inhibitor removal.
   1.4 Initial amount & purity: mass or mol; % purity; internal standard (if any).

2. Catalyst system
   2.1 Metal/precursor: metal (Rh/Co/Ru/Ir/Pd/Pt/Fe/Ni); precatalyst (e.g., Rh(acac)(CO)₂, HRh(CO)(PPh₃)₃, Co₂(CO)₈); loading (mol%, mmol, ppm); preformation (in situ/ex situ/orthometalated); co-metals/promoters (Re/Mo/W/Co).
   2.2 Ligand(s): type (phosphine/phosphite/…/NHC); denticity (mono/bi/tri/tetra); chirality (absolute config); family tag (e.g., Xantphos, BIPHEPHOS, TPPTS); L\:M ratio (initial/maintained); descriptors (Tolman cone angle, TEP, bite angle).
   2.3 Solubility/phase & supports: organic phase, water-soluble (TPPTS), ionic liquid, polymer-anchored, immobilized.
   2.4 Stabilizers & additives to catalyst: amines/epoxide/HALS, amounts; notes on ligand susceptibility (oxidation/hydrolysis).
   2.5 Recycle/handling: strategy (gas-recycle stripping, distillative recycle, aqueous biphasic, IL partition, membrane, immobilized); Rh/Co loss per cycle; lifetime metrics; decobalting/recovery steps.

3. Reactor & process configuration
   3.1 Reactor type & material: batch autoclave/CSTR/flow loop/gas-recycle/tube/microwave; stainless/Hastelloy/glass-liner.
   3.2 Scale & hydrodynamics: working volume, fill fraction, stirring/impeller, mixing notes.
   3.3 Gas handling: single-shot charge vs continuous feed; MFC settings; leak test; gas recycle/compression.
   3.4 Phase mode & in-line separation: single organic, aqueous biphasic, IL biphasic, micellar, scCO₂; on-line stripping/distillation, phase split, filtration/membrane.

4. Reaction conditions
   4.1 Solvent system: identity, amount, dryness; or neat operation.
   4.2 Temperature profile: setpoint(s), ramp, hold.
   4.3 Pressure & composition: P\_total; pCO, pH₂; CO\:H₂ ratio (bar or vol%).
   4.4 Gas alternatives: syngas-free routes (formaldehyde/formic acid, CO₂+H₂/RWGS); generation conditions.
   4.5 Concentrations & ratios: catalyst concentration (mM/ppm), substrate\:metal ratio.
   4.6 Additives: bases/acids/salts/water/epoxides; purpose & loading.
   4.7 Time & sampling: total time, sampling schedule.
   4.8 Poisons/inhibitors present & mitigation.

5. Analytics & in/at-line monitoring
   5.1 On-line: IR/ATR-FTIR (νCO bands), in situ IR autoclave, mass-spec.
   5.2 Off-line: GC/FID (column, program, internal standard), GC–MS, HPLC; NMR (^31P for speciation; ^1H/^13C for products); UV-Vis/CD (preformation).
   5.3 Reporting basis & quantitation: calibration approach; response factors; detection limits.

6. Results (per experiment)
   6.1 Conversion & yields: conversion (%); product yields for n-aldehyde, iso-aldehyde(s), hydrogenated alkane/alcohol, isomerized olefins, unsaturated aldehydes, others.
   6.2 Regio/chemo/stereoselectivity: l/b ratio; aldehyde vs alcohol vs alkane; diastereomeric ratio; enantiomeric excess (ee%).
   6.3 Kinetics & productivity: TOF (definition, h⁻¹); TON (per run & cumulative); initial rate; activation/pressure dependencies (Ea, orders) if given.
   6.4 Stability & reuse: time-on-stream, cycles; speciation during run (ligand loss, Rh clusters, Co carbide); post-run characterization (IR-CO, ^31P NMR, plating).

7. Controls & diagnostics
   7.1 Hydrogenation-only (H₂, no CO) and CO-only controls; leak/dark tests.
   7.2 Isomerization snapshots (low pCO/high pH₂); substrate-poison challenges (butadiene/alkyne/S/Cl tolerance).
   7.3 Ligand degradation tests (oxidation/hydrolysis) with and without stabilizers.

8. Mechanistic & descriptor data (if reported)
   8.1 Observed intermediates: RhH(CO)₂L₂, acyl-Rh, Co-carbonyl species; IR νCO values and assignments.
   8.2 Computational/microkinetic descriptors: ΔG‡ trends; maps correlating TEP/cone/bite with l/b or rate.

9. Downstream/tandem chemistry
   9.1 Tandem type: hydroaminomethylation; aldol→hydrogenation; cyclohydrocarbonylation; dehydration; other.
   9.2 Follow-up conditions & overall yields across steps.

10. Process & scale-up specifics
    10.1 Generation/era: Co high-P (2nd gen), Rh LPO (3rd gen), aqueous two-phase TPPTS (4th gen).
    10.2 Throughput & campaign: mass/throughput; hours on stream; uptime.
    10.3 Workup & recycle: gas-recycle/distillative; decobalting; metal losses.
    10.4 Safety: CO handling, interlocks, reliefs, monitoring.
